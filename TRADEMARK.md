## Permitted uses

You may use DealDesk marks without prior written permission when all of the
following are true:

1. **Nominative reference** — You need the name to truthfully describe what
   you are talking about (for example: "a fork of DealDesk" or "compatible
   with the DealDesk API").
2. **No endorsement implied** — You do not suggest sponsorship, affiliation,
   or approval by DealDesk AI.
3. **No confusion** — Your product, company, domain, or npm package name is
   clearly distinct from official DealDesk offerings.
4. **Accurate attribution** — Forks and derivatives clearly state they are
   modified versions and not the official project.

Examples that are generally fine:

- "Built on DealDesk"
- "DealDesk plugin for Acme CRM"
- "Unofficial DealDesk Docker image"
- Describing compatibility with the `dealdesk` CLI or `@dealdesk/*` packages

## Uses that require permission

Contact DealDesk AI before:

- Using **DealDesk** or confusingly similar names in a
  **product**, **company**, **domain**, or **public npm package** name
- Using official logos or icons in a way that implies endorsement
- Marketing a hosted service as "DealDesk" without clarifying it is operated
  by a third party
- Registering a trademark that incorporates or closely resembles our marks

When in doubt, pick a distinct name and use language like "for DealDesk" or
"DealDesk-compatible" instead of adopting our marks as your brand.
